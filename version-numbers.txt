Managing TestFlight builds without a full review for every update is a common goal for developers. You don’t need to use a weird version number like `0.0.0.1` to achieve this; it actually comes down to how you handle **Version Numbers** vs. **Build Numbers**.

### The Core Rule

Apple requires a "Beta App Review" for the **first build** of every new **Version Number** you send to external testers. Once that version is approved, subsequent updates using the **same Version Number** (but a higher Build Number) usually bypass the manual review or undergo a much faster, automated check.

| Term | In `Info.plist` | Example | Role in Review |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Version Number** | `CFBundleShortVersionString` | `1.2.0` | Triggers a **New Review** when changed. |
| **Build Number** | `CFBundleVersion` | `45` | Can be updated **without** a new review. |

---

### How to Skip the Review

To push updates immediately to your external testers:

1. **Keep the Version Number constant:** If your currently approved TestFlight version is `1.2.0`, keep it at `1.2.0` for your next update.
2. **Increment the Build Number:** Change the build from `1` to `2` (or `45` to `46`).
3. **Submit for Review:** When you submit this new build in App Store Connect, Apple will ask: *"Have there been any significant changes since your last build?"*
4. **Select "No":** If you select "No," the build is typically approved for external testing **instantly** or within minutes.

### The "High Version" Strategy

The "0.0.0.1" or "99.9.9" strategy you've seen is used by developers who want to keep their TestFlight track entirely separate from their App Store track.

* **The Problem:** Once you release `1.2.0` to the actual App Store, you can no longer push new builds to `1.2.0` in TestFlight. You are forced to move to `1.2.1` or `1.3.0`, which triggers a fresh review.
* **The Workaround:** Some developers use a version like `9.9.9` in TestFlight. Since they never "release" version `9.9.9` to the App Store, they can keep adding Build `1, 2, 3... 500` to it indefinitely without ever changing the version number and triggering a full re-review.

> [!TIP]
> **Internal Testers** (members of your App Store Connect team) never require a review. If you have a small group of trusted testers, add them as "Internal" to get updates the second they finish processing.

### Limits to Watch Out For

* **Build Expiration:** Builds expire after **90 days**. Even if you stay on the same version number, you'll eventually have to bump it when that 90-day window closes.
* **Rate Limiting:** If you submit too many builds in a single day (usually more than 2-3), Apple may flag the build for a manual "random" review anyway.
